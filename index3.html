Volgograd (until 1925 - Tsaritsyn, until 1961 - Stalingrad) is a city in the southeast of the European part of Russia, the administrative center of the Volgograd region. Volgograd River Terminal is the first thing that catches your eye if you find yourself on the embankment of this city. This is a huge building, comparable in length to Moscow's Red Square - almost 300 meters. Today it is the largest building of this type in Europe.

The port can simultaneously service 6 motor ships and accommodate 700 people in the waiting room, where you can also find various boutiques, cafes and restaurants. Today, the building also houses a philharmonic society designed for 1,000 people and a technological institute.

Battle of Stalingrad Save to My Places The city's main museum is dedicated to the key battle of the Great Patriotic War -- the Battle of Stalingrad. The exhibition is located on the site of the Tsaritsyn Defense Museum, which operated since 1937. In its current form, the exhibition opened on May 31, 1982, according to the design of the chief architect of Volgograd.

 

Pavlov's House

Save to My Places

A couple of minutes' walk from the Battle of Stalingrad Museum is a historic building - Pavlov's House. This residential building built in the 1930s was one of the most prestigious in pre-war Stalingrad. During the battle, a group of soldiers led by Yakov Pavlov spent time in it, and they were able to hold the line for 58 days.
